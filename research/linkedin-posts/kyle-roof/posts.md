## Post 1: "EEAT and the Future of SEO with AI"
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/in/kyleroof/ (collected from podcast and LinkedIn, 2023)  
**Theme:** EEAT as mandatory AI-era signal

**Key excerpt:**
> "E-E-A-T is not a nice-to-have in the age of AI. It's mandatory. When every piece of content can be written by AI, Google's only way to differentiate is to look at signals of real expertise: who wrote this? Do they have a track record? Are they cited by authoritative sources? Is there evidence of genuine experience? ## Post 2: "400+ SEO Tests — What I've Learned"
**Theme:** Data-driven SEO vs. opinion-based SEO  
**Collected from:** Kyle Roof LinkedIn + podcast interviews, 2024

**Key excerpt:**
> "I've run over 400 controlled SEO tests. Here's what I've confirmed through testing that contradicts what most 'experts' say: 1) Writing quality has much less impact on rankings than technical on-page signals. 2) AI content can rank if the technical signals are correct. 3) EEAT signals (author bio, credentials, citations) DO have measurable ranking impact. 4) Content length matters less than content completeness on the specific topic. 5) Internal linking structure is massively underrated."

**Why this matters:** Separates confirmed SEO signal from speculation — directly relevant for building AI content workflows that are grounded in evidence.

## Post 3: "I Ranked a Page to #1 Using Lorem Ipsum"
**Theme:** On-page technical signals vs. writing quality  
**Collected from:** Kyle Roof interviews and LinkedIn, 2022–2024

**Key excerpt:**
> "Years ago, I ranked a page to position #1 using Lorem Ipsum — no real content at all, just optimized on-page technical signals. This wasn't to say content doesn't matter. It was to prove that technical on-page optimization is dramatically more powerful than most people realize. The implication for AI content: technically optimized AI content will often outrank well-written content that ignores technical SEO. Build the technical foundation first."

**Why this matters:** The most compelling argument for why AI-generated content, when properly technically optimized, can be competitive with manually written content.

## Post 4: "SEO Ranking Factors for 2025 — What's Actually Working"
**Theme:** Current ranking factor landscape  
**Collected from:** YouTube/LinkedIn, July 2025

**Key excerpt:**
> "What's actually working in 2025 vs. what people think is working: WORKING: topical depth, E-E-A-T signals, entity optimization, technical on-page, internal linking architecture. NOT WORKING: raw content volume without quality signals, thin AI content without author credentials, keyword stuffing in any form, link farms. The signal has gotten more sophisticated, not less. More content means more noise, which means Google has gotten better at filtering it."

**Why this matters:** 2025-specific validation of what the content production system should optimize for.

## Post 5: "Future-Proofing SEO Against AI Disruption"
**Theme:** Long-term SEO strategy in the AI era  
**Collected from:** Page Optimizer Pro content + LinkedIn, 2024

**Key excerpt:**
> "Future-proof SEO is not about chasing the latest algorithm update. It's about building things that Google has always rewarded and always will: genuine expertise, comprehensive coverage of a topic, real user value, and trustworthy author signals. AI can help you produce this at scale — but only if the foundation is real. AI that produces expertise signals that don't exist is fraud, and Google will eventually detect it."
