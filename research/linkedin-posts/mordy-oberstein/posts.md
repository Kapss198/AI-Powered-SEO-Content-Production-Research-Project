## Post 1: "The Commodity Content Trap"
**Theme:** AI content quality signals  
**Collected from:** Mordy Oberstein LinkedIn + SERP's Up podcast, 2024–2025

**Key excerpt:**
> "At Wix, we can see what's happening across millions of sites. The pattern is clear: sites that flooded Google with AI-generated commodity content saw short-term gains followed by severe drops. The sites that used AI to improve the quality and depth of their content — not just the volume — held their positions. Google is not penalizing AI content. It's penalizing commodity content. The two are not the same, but they often overlap."

**Why this matters:** Aggregate data from Wix (millions of sites) directly validates the quality-over-volume principle — the most empirically grounded data point on AI content SEO performance.

## Post 2: "What Google's Algorithm Actually Responds To in 2025"
**Theme:** Ranking factor realities  
**Collected from:** SERP's Up podcast + LinkedIn, 2025

**Key excerpt:**
> "Based on what I see across Wix's data: Google's 2025 algorithm is heavily rewarding content that: 1) Earns genuine user engagement (time on page, return visits). 2) Has a consistent publishing pattern — not burst publishing. 3) Covers topics comprehensively, not just broadly. 4) Has strong author identity and credential signals. 5) Gets shared and referenced outside of Google. The 'write and forget' model is dead. The 'publish at scale and see what sticks' model is dying."

**Why this matters:** Multi-site data validation of what signals actually work — directly challenges the "more is better" AI content production approach.

## Post 3: "SEO Branding in the Age of AI"
**Theme:** Brand as SEO signal  
**Collected from:** LinkedIn post + interview, 2023

**Key excerpt:**
> "Your brand is now an SEO signal. When users search your brand name, when they engage with your branded content, when they come back to your site directly — all of this tells Google your content is worth surfacing. AI-generated content that doesn't build brand memory is SEO that works once. Content that builds a brand that people remember and search for is SEO that compounds. The brand IS the moat."

**Why this matters:** Reframes content strategy from keyword targeting to brand-building — essential context for why AI content production without brand identity fails long-term.

## Post 4: "The March 2024 Core Update: What Actually Happened"
**Theme:** Algorithm update analysis  
**Collected from:** Mordy Oberstein LinkedIn + SERP's Up podcast, 2024

**Key excerpt:**
> "The March 2024 Core Update didn't target AI content specifically. It targeted low-quality content at scale — which happened to be mostly AI-generated. The sites that got hit weren't penalized for using AI. They were penalized for producing content that added no new information, had no real editorial perspective, and was clearly written for rankings rather than readers. Google didn't change its quality standards. It got better at enforcing them."

**Why this matters:** Corrects the narrative that AI content is banned — clarifies that it's commodity content that's penalized, not AI content per se.

## Post 5: "Talking to Mordy: The Future of AI Content Quality"
**Theme:** AI search and content evolution  
**Collected from:** Mordy Oberstein interview + LinkedIn, 2025

**Key excerpt:**
> "The future state I see coming: AI will be used to identify content gaps and produce first drafts, but the competitive differentiator will be the editorial layer on top — the opinion, the original research, the voice. The ones that have just AI workflows will produce content that all looks and sounds the same — and Google, and AI answer engines, will rank the differentiated over the homogeneous."

**Why this matters:** Forward-looking prediction from a data-grounded position — identifies editorial differentiation as the durable competitive advantage in AI content production.
