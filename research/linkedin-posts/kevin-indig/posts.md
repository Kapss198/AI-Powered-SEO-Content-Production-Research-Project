## Post 1: "Commodity Content vs Defensible Content"
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_ai-content-seo-activity-7137469480791621633-XCxZ  
**Theme:** AI content defensibility

**Key excerpt:**
> "If your content is easy to copy, you're dealing with commodities. If your content is hard to copy, your marketing strategy is defensible. AI drives the value of commodity content to zero. Smart marketers are either (1) technical enough to enrich AI content with enough data to make it unique OR (2) focused on differentiation instead. One is a better version of the same game. The other is a different game entirely."

**Why this matters:** The clearest framework for deciding what AI-powered content production is worth doing vs. what will be competed away.

## Post 3: "The Human-AI Hybrid Content Stack"
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_seo-aiwriter-ai-activity-7075494996606390274-3hRO  
**Theme:** AI content workflow design

**Key excerpt:**
> "Even with AI, you still need people to: validate the information, ensure it doesn't sound like a robot, layer in unique insights and data, and make strategic decisions about what to publish. The best content strategies in the near future are human/AI hybrid approaches. Pure AI output is commodity. Pure human output at scale is impossible. The hybrid is where the leverage lives."

**Why this matters:** Practical workflow architecture — identifies exactly where human effort is irreplaceable in an AI content pipeline.

## Post 4: "Programmatic SEO in the AI Era"
**URL:** https://www.linkedin.com/posts/kevinindig_seo-programmaticseo-ai-activity-7082354507581444097-2uT8  
**Theme:** Programmatic SEO evolution

**Key excerpt:**
> "Programmatic SEO was supposed to be the future: create 10,000 pages targeting 10,000 long-tail keywords at minimal cost. Then AI happened. Now anyone can create 10,000 pages. The barrier is gone. So the moat shifts: from 'can you create at scale?' to 'can you create at scale AND be cited as authoritative?' Data + distribution + brand trust are the new programmatic moat."

**Why this matters:** Identifies the post-AI shift in programmatic SEO — what used to be a volume game is now a quality/authority game.
